# Business Model

*This document describes a plausible business model for Earth Guardian AI as
a pitch/planning exercise. It is not a claim that this company, its revenue,
or its customer base currently exist.*

## Value Proposition

One environmental intelligence layer instead of a dozen disconnected tools —
sold as a subscription to organizations that need to monitor and report on
environmental risk, and offered free to individuals and students.

## Customer Segments

1. **City & regional governments** — sustainability offices, emergency
   management agencies
2. **Enterprises** — ESG/sustainability reporting teams, supply-chain risk teams
3. **NGOs & research institutions** — discounted/free access in exchange for
   data-sharing partnerships
4. **Individuals** — free tier (carbon calculator, learning hub, city risk lookup)

## Revenue Streams

| Stream | Description | Target segment |
|---|---|---|
| SaaS subscription | Tiered dashboard access (Executive / Investor / Government / Research) | Governments, enterprises |
| API licensing | Programmatic access to composite risk scores and forecasts | Developers, other platforms |
| Data partnerships | Aggregated, anonymized insight-sharing agreements | Research institutions |
| Grants & philanthropic funding | Climate-tech and disaster-resilience grant programs | Non-profit arm |
| White-label deployments | Branded instances for governments or NGOs | Public sector |

## Pricing Sketch (illustrative)

- **Free** — individual carbon calculator, learning hub, public risk map
- **Team ($499/mo)** — full dashboard suite for a single city or organization
- **Enterprise (custom)** — multi-region deployment, SLA, dedicated data pipeline, API access

## Go-to-Market

1. Launch as an open, well-documented demo (this repository) to build
   credibility with developers and climate-tech communities
2. Partner with 2–3 mid-sized cities or NGOs as design partners for the first
   real data integrations
3. Apply to climate-tech accelerators and competitions (see
   [AWARDS_AND_COMPETITIONS.md](AWARDS_AND_COMPETITIONS.md)) for early
   validation and non-dilutive funding
4. Expand from a single flagship region to a global city index

## Competitive Landscape

Adjacent categories include climate risk analytics (e.g. enterprise climate
risk scoring vendors), disaster-alerting platforms, and ESG reporting
software. Earth Guardian AI's differentiation is breadth (14 modules under one
UX) and accessibility (a genuinely usable, good-looking product rather than a
spreadsheet-and-PDF-report workflow).

## Key Risks

- Data licensing costs for premium satellite/sensor feeds could be significant
- Government procurement cycles are slow — enterprise/NGO segments may be
  faster early revenue
- Model accuracy and liability: disaster-prediction claims require careful
  legal and scientific validation before being sold as decision-grade
