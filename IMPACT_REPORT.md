# Impact Report (Illustrative)

*This is a template/projection document for how Earth Guardian AI would frame
its impact once deployed with real users and real data — written here as a
planning artifact, not a report of achieved results.*

## Theory of Change

Faster, clearer access to environmental risk information → faster institutional
and individual decisions → reduced harm from climate and environmental
disasters, and accelerated adoption of sustainable practices.

```
Fragmented data  →  Unified intelligence layer  →  Faster decisions  →  Reduced harm
  (today's           (Earth Guardian AI)            (governments,        + accelerated
   problem)                                           NGOs, individuals)    sustainability
```

## Target Impact Metrics (once live)

| Metric | How it would be measured |
|---|---|
| Lead time on disaster alerts | Hours of advance warning delivered vs. baseline agency alerts |
| Cities onboarded | Count of municipal partners using the platform |
| Carbon footprint reduction | Aggregate self-reported reduction via the calculator's recommendations |
| Learning engagement | Course completions on the Sustainability Learning Hub |
| Volunteer hours mobilized | Hours logged through NGO Network partner campaigns |

## Alignment with Global Frameworks

Earth Guardian AI's module set maps onto several UN Sustainable Development
Goals:

- **SDG 13 — Climate Action** (Climate Intelligence, Disaster Prediction)
- **SDG 6 — Clean Water and Sanitation** (Water Security)
- **SDG 7 — Affordable and Clean Energy** (Renewable Energy Intelligence)
- **SDG 11 — Sustainable Cities and Communities** (Smart Cities)
- **SDG 14 & 15 — Life Below Water / Life on Land** (Ocean Protection, Forest & Biodiversity)
- **SDG 3 — Good Health and Well-Being** (Air Quality health-impact framing)

## Honest Caveat

No impact has been measured yet because the platform, as shipped in this
repository, runs on mock data and has no live user base. This document exists
to show how impact *would* be tracked and reported — useful for grant
applications, competition submissions, and internal planning — not as a claim
of results already achieved.
